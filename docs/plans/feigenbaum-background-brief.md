## Who Is Rabbi Yitzchak Feigenbaum?

**Background:**
- Born in Cleveland, Ohio
- Trained under HaGaon HaRav Beinush Finkel, Rosh Yeshiva of Mir Jerusalem
- Co-founded Yeshivat Darche Noam / David Shapell College of Jewish Studies (1978) — a yeshiva built for university-age men with little formal Jewish background
- Was the **first Mashgiach at Aish HaTorah Jerusalem** (foundational figure in the BT movement)
- Moved to Toronto in 1987; taught at Jewish high schools, lectured for Aish, Ohr Somayach, and NCSY
- Founded **Tiferes Bais Yaakov High School** (girls' division) in 2000
- Runs **Shiras Sarah** (rabbiyfeigenbaum.com) — an organization training educators to teach hashkafah to students

**Why this matters for your meeting:**
Rabbi Feigenbaum has spent 40+ years solving exactly the problem DavenAlong addresses: helping people who are disconnected from Jewish practice find their way in. He's not an abstract Torah scholar — he built institutions for it. The app is a natural extension of his life's work.

## The Feigenbaum Siddur

**Publication:** February 2022  
**Publisher:** Feldheim Publishers (primary); also distributed by Mosaica Press  
**Editions:** Weekday (Ashkenaz & Sefard) and Shabbos (Ashkenaz & Sefard)

### Pedagogical Approach

The siddur was designed **explicitly for teenagers** — but has been adopted widely by adults seeking deeper engagement with davening. Key design choices:

1. **Modern, accessible English translation**  
   Written in clear, everyday language. Not liturgical or formal — readable by someone who has never opened a siddur before.

2. **FAQ feature throughout the text**  
   This is his signature innovation. Embedded FAQs address "hashkafah questions that many of us have today, but might be hesitant to ask." Direct, honest, down-to-earth answers. No other major siddur does this.

3. **Meaning-focused, not reference-focused**  
   Unlike ArtScroll (comprehensive annotations, halachic instruction) or Koren (scholarly commentary, Modern Orthodox context), Feigenbaum's siddur optimizes for understanding *what you're saying right now* — not what you might look up later.

4. **Concentration and kavannah as outcomes**  
   The design goal is a "more personal conversation with Hashem." Multiple testimonials confirm that embedded commentary helps users stay focused rather than losing their place.

### How It Compares to Other Siddurim

| Siddur | Audience | Approach | Tone |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Feigenbaum** | Teens + returning adults | FAQ-driven, pedagogical | Modern, personal |
| ArtScroll (Scherman) | General observant | Comprehensive reference | Formal, scholarly |
| Koren Sacks | Modern Orthodox adult | Literary, thoughtful | Intellectual |
| Artscroll Teen | Teens | Simplified ArtScroll | Accessible but not innovative |

Feigenbaum's FAQ feature has no equivalent in any other major siddur. It's the single most distinctive element — and it maps directly onto the dual beginner/returning entry path that DavenAlong is building.

## The Alignment With DavenAlong

The overlap between what Feigenbaum designed and what DavenAlong is building is unusually strong:

| Feigenbaum Siddur | DavenAlong Feature |
|---|---|
| Written for people who don't know the words | Dual entry path: beginner vs. returning |
| Modern English translation | Three-layer display: Hebrew / transliteration / English |
| FAQ answering "why" questions during davening | SectionIntro — contextual explanation before each section |
| Meaning over mechanics | Continuous scroll — no page-tapping, unbroken flow |
| Built for concentration and kavannah | ZmanimHeader — time context so user can focus, not calculate |

When you walk him through the app, this alignment is the story you're telling. Not "we built an app that uses your text" but "we built the delivery layer your siddur was always asking for."

## Talking Points for the Meeting

**Opening context:**
- He co-founded Darche Noam in 1978 — the year before Rob was born. He's been doing this for a long time.
- Aish HaTorah connection is worth one sentence of acknowledgment if it comes up naturally.
- Don't over-reference his bio. Just know it.

**The question his siddur answers:**
His siddur is built around the question: *"How do we help someone who wants to daven but keeps losing the thread?"* His answer was modern language + FAQs + commentary on meaning.

DavenAlong's answer is: a continuous scroll that doesn't break the flow, a ZmanimHeader that removes cognitive load, and a dual entry path that meets users where they are.

Frame it as: *"You solved the content problem. We're trying to solve the delivery problem."*

**The FAQ feature:**
This is worth asking about directly:
- *"The FAQ feature in your siddur — what kinds of questions does it answer? And do you find that people actually engage with those, or do they skip them?"*
- His answer will tell you how central the "why" framing is to his vision. If it's core → suggest that DavenAlong could surface FAQ-style notes in the SectionIntro component.

**Publisher awareness:**
He publishes through Feldheim. Licensing negotiations will likely involve Feldheim. You may want to ask: *"If we moved forward, would I be speaking with you, or would that conversation involve Feldheim directly?"* — so you know the right path.
