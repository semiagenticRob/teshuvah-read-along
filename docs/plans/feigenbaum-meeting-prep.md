# DavenAlong — Feigenbaum Meeting Prep
## May 10, 2026

Written by CEO Agent, 2026-04-25.

---

## Context

Rabbi Feigenbaum is the author of the siddur DavenAlong is building toward. His siddur is the recommended source; the TJE rabbis made the intro directly. This is a warm partnership conversation, not a cold pitch. The relationship is already established through Rabbi Leban and the TJE community.

**The ask:** Content licensing + rabbinic authority. The app can deliver the siddur experience at scale — but only with the right content.

---

## Meeting Objective

Leave with one of three outcomes, in order of preference:

1. **Partnership agreement in principle** — Feigenbaum content licensed (even informally), DavenAlong credited as an authorized companion app
2. **Next step committed** — Specific follow-up scheduled (e.g., call with his publisher, review of the app build, intro to relevant staff)
3. **Honest door left open** — He understands what you're building, is supportive, agrees to review a working prototype once the continuous scroll build is complete

---

## Before the Meeting

- [ ] Send the short version of the partnership email (docs/plans/feigenbaum-partnership-email.md) as a pre-meeting primer 3-5 days before May 10
- [ ] Confirm the ShacharitScrollScreen is navigable and demo-able on a real device (not simulator)

## Meeting Structure (45-60 minutes)

### Opening — 5 minutes

Start with gratitude and the personal connection:

> "I want to start by thanking you for making time. Rabbi [Leban/intro rabbi] speaks so highly of your work — and once I understood what you built with your siddur, I realized there was no other version of this conversation to have."

State intent up front: you're not here to sell, you're here to understand if there's a shared vision.

---

### Part 1: Understand His World — 10 minutes

Before showing anything, ask questions. Let him talk.

**Key questions:**

1. *"What's the primary audience you wrote the siddur for? Is it primarily the returning Jew, or did you design it to also serve someone who davens every day?"*

2. *"When you think about people who own your siddur — what's the most common thing they struggle with? Is it the Hebrew, the pace, knowing when to pause?"*

3. *"Has there been any prior interest in digital versions of your work? What happened with those conversations?"*

4. *"What would concern you most about a digital version of davening? What would you want to make sure it got right?"*

Listen carefully. His answers reframe your pitch. If his primary concern is authenticity → lead with the ZmanimHeader and the beginner/returning dual path. If his concern is pace → lead with the continuous scroll and audio synchronization. If he's had bad prior experiences → address those directly.

---

### Part 2: Show the App — 15 minutes

Do not just demo. Walk him through it as a user would experience it.

**Demo flow:**

1. Open the app on a real device. Say: *"I'll show you what exists today — this is not a mockup."*

2. Start on the EntryPath screen (if built) or the main menu. Say: *"There are two entry points — one for someone coming back to davening after years away, and one for someone who already knows the words. Rabbi [Leban] told me that gap is where most people fall through."*

3. Navigate into ShacharitScrollScreen. Let him watch the continuous scroll for a moment without narrating. Then: *"This is continuous scroll — the entire Shacharit as one unbroken experience. You don't tap through pages or break the flow."*

4. Point to where ZmanimHeader will appear (even if not built yet). Say: *"Right here, the app will show today's zmanim — when the latest zman Shema is, when davening time expires. So someone who's rushing knows exactly how to pace themselves."*

5. Toggle the Hebrew/transliteration/English display if built. Say: *"Every word has three layers — the person who wants only Hebrew, the person who needs transliteration, and the person who reads English. The reader controls their own level."*

**After the demo:** Ask him — don't tell him.

> *"Does this match the way you think about the problem? What would you change about what you saw?"*

---

### Part 3: The Partnership Conversation — 15 minutes

Frame it as alignment, not a transaction:

> *"What I'd love to explore with you is whether there's a version of this that carries your name and your siddur's structure — so that when someone opens this app, they're engaging with your work, not a generic prayer text. You've already done the hardest part: the pedagogy. I want to build the delivery layer."*

**Core ask:**

> *"What I'm asking for today is: would you be willing to let me build a version of DavenAlong using your siddur's content and structure? I want to attribute it fully, bring it to you for review before any public launch, and structure any commercial terms in a way that respects your work."*

**Likely responses and how to handle:**

| His response | Your reply |
|---|---|
| "I'd need to speak with my publisher" | "Of course. Would you be willing to make that introduction? I can put together whatever they need in writing." |
| "What does 'commercial terms' mean for something like this?" | "I haven't figured out the exact model yet — the app is free with an optional Pro tier. I'd want your input on what feels right, including whether you'd prefer a flat licensing fee, a share of Pro revenue, or no payment and just attribution." |
| "I'd need to see a more finished product first" | "That's completely fair. The remaining build is ZmanimHeader and the dual entry path — I can have those complete within 2-3 weeks. Can we schedule a follow-up once those are done?" |
| "I'm not sure this is right for the audience" | "Tell me more — what's your concern? I want to understand what would need to be true for it to feel right." |
| Immediate yes | Shake on it. Ask: "What's your preferred way to document this — a simple email, or should I put together a short one-page term sheet?" |

---

### Part 4: Close and Next Steps — 5 minutes

Do not leave without a specific next action on the table.

Options to offer:

- *"Can I send you the written pitch so you can share it with your publisher?"*
- *"Would you be willing to do a 30-minute follow-up call once the ZmanimHeader build is complete?"*
- *"Can I send you a TestFlight link so you can try the app yourself before we talk next?"*

Confirm the next action out loud before you leave: *"So — I'll send the longer version of the written pitch by [date], and we'll connect again by [date]. Does that work?"*

## What Success Looks Like

| Outcome | What it means |
|---|---|
| Partnership in principle ✓ | Proceed with Feigenbaum content layer. Update reps.yaml: status → live imminent. |
| Follow-up call committed ✓ | Ship ZmanimHeader + EntryPath within 2 weeks, then schedule demo. |
| Intro to publisher ✓ | Send formal proposal. Engage publisher on licensing terms. |
| Needs more time / wants to think | Send short pitch email within 24h. Follow up in 2 weeks. |
| Not interested | Pivot to open-source siddur content (Sefaria, Artscroll public domain). App continues without exclusive partnership. |

---

## Things NOT to Do

- Do not mention specific revenue projections or user targets — the app is pre-launch with no traction yet
- Do not promise a specific ship date unless you're confident
- Do not pitch the business first — lead with the mission and the user (the person coming back to davening)
- Do not let the meeting run past 60 minutes without a committed next step
